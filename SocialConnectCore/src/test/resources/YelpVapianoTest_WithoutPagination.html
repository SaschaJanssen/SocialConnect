    Even though Vapiano is one of my favorite places to go because of its uniqueness, grand decor, wonderful atmosphere, great atmosphere, it still only gets 4 stars.

    To those new to Vapiano, this is the place to go for a nice Italian dinner, or just a place to get drinks and chill. You get personal pay cards as you walk in so it takes away the awkwardness of splitting the bill. Those cards track your personal orders and you hand it over to pay in the end. Stingy friends will make that difficult but with the personal pay cards, you can have a relaxing night without having to deal with those issues.   Another unique trait about this place is that everything you order is straight off and fresh from the shelves behind the cooks.

    We ordered an Americana pizza (with  Tomato Sauce, Mozarella, Spicy Italian Sausage, Sliced Sicilian Pepperoni) Because everything is fresh, the pizza was piping hot when we got it. Love it!  

    Presentation: Pizzas these sizes are made to share.  Decent sized but not too large. Presentation was alright and nothing to brag about. The crust was too wide. The chefs could have expanded the sauce, meat,  and sausage more-- up to 1.5 inches from the edge of the crust.  One side of the pizza look like it was smushed inwards and the slices were not equal at all. Some slices were significantly bigger than others. I suggest so also make the crust a bit thicker so it's not flimsy when you pick it up, add more sausage bits and pepperoni as well.  

    Taste: DESPITE the OKAY presentation, it tasted great. The sauce was wonderful. It balanced tangy, sweet, and salty taste that makes every bite flavorful with the cheese, sausage, and pepperoni.  I prefer that the sausage bits to be bigger too. It tasted like a regular cheese pizza every now and then. Overall, I enjoyed the pizza and would like to try another pizza and some pasta dishes next time.

    Vapiano always has good service but could smile some more and be friendlier to make it a better experience for patrons. Some staff were more attentive, friendly, and professional than others.

    New York, NY

    Date of Last Visit: July 15, 2012

    The Situation: Quick catch up dinner after a long day of Sunday errands. Though I've been here before for catch up dinners during the week as well.

    The Scene: Combination of tourists, students, families and people looking for a casual dinner. Oddly crowded during the week.  

    If you haven't been there, it's basically a restaurant decor set up with a cafeteria
    style set up.

    When you walk in, you get a card that you carry around that gets charged at each 'station' you visit, whether it's the bar, the pasta spot, the pizza spot, etc.

    It's an interesting concept because the idea, as what their official website states, is to avoid bill confusion and to cut down on the amount of time you're waiting on the bill.

    You can have one card for your party or separate cards for each person in your party.

    You choose your own seating.

    The Staff & Such: The hostesses were welcoming and the bartenders were friendly as well as the cooks. But when it gets a bit crowded, some of the staff doesn't quite get that maybe there needs to be extra effort involved.

    Example: There were only two lines for pasta and as each respective line began to build up, the first staff response was to give out free wine...to 1/3 of the people on each line. Um, hello, did you ever learn "bring enough for everyone" from school?

    So about 10-15 minutes after the failed attempt at pacifying grumbling customers, they decide to open a 3rd line.

    And in classic grocery store fashion, I move to the 3rd line..only to realize this cook is actually somehow slower than the cook that I was waiting for on the previous line *sigh*.

    You also have to get your own Tupperware if you want to bring home leftovers, which is fine except that the cook whom I asked actually asked me if I was Asian before he would give it to me. That was my WTF moment. Um, why is this relevant? Not certain if this was a bad attempt at humor.

    Food: If you want good casual Italian food, go to an Italian restaurant. (If you are in the area, Michael's comes to mind. )

    However, if you are looking for reasonably priced Italian food beyond delis and decent pizza in a nicely decorated spot and don't mind the cafeteria style set up, this spot is for you.

    This visit I had the Carpaccio Antipasti (thinly sliced filet of beef, arugula, mushrooms, parmesan, cipriani sauce). It was quite yummy and a large amount of food on the plate. For the pasta I had the Pomodoro e Mozzarella with the tagliatelle and cherry tomatoes. Took a bit of time but quite delish considering the venue.

    Alcohol - Being in summer mode, I opted for the red sangria. It was tasty but a bit weak. After checking in on FourSquare, I took advantage of the Aperol Spritz special despite the "Robitussin" remark I saw on the tips page.

    I don't think it was that bad but it definitely needed a little more of something. I switched over to Malbec for the night which worked out well.

    The Threads: Casual.

    Cannot wait to go back!!!

    A friend suggested Vapiano for a late Saturday lunch. Around 3pm on a sunny summer day, it wasn't too crowded. I love the space, with vaulted ceilings, a bar and lounge right up front, and tons of seating. The vibe is young, fresh, modern...even a little sexy.

    Vapiano is a little bit like Hill Country BBQ, where each person (or group, depending on how you want to pay), gets their own card which is tapped at stations in the restaurant, eventually totaling your purchases on your way OUT of the restaurant. Along the back of the restaurant are stations for Salads, Brick-Oven Pizza, Pasta, etc...all incredibly fresh, and made to order right in front of your eyes. I tried the Shrimp Scampi pasta for $12.95 and it was amazing...nice portion size, all hot and fresh, and I could have as much Parmesan cheese on top as I wanted. Along with the pasta came three slices of fresh Italian bread. I didn't try the salads or pizza, though I was tempted. I did however, get a glass of white zinfandel at the bar for $6.50, which went very well with my meal.

    My friend and I sat upstairs, which is a little quieter than the main room downstairs. Though getting up and down the stairs with a tray is a little difficult, once we were up there it was worth the trip.

    Vapiano is great for when you are dining alone but want a non-fast food meal, for travelers and students on a budget, and for groups where people prefer to pay for their own meals rather than splitting a check.  I can't wait to recommend it to my book club!
